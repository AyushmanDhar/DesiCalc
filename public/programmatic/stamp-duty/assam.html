Overview of Real Estate in Assam

Assam, the gateway to Northeast India, has a dynamic real estate market driven by urbanisation, infrastructure development, and the growth of Guwahati as a commercial hub. The state capital Dispur, along with Guwahati city, forms the primary real estate destination, accounting for the majority of registered property transactions in the state. Other emerging cities include Jorhat, Silchar, Dibrugarh, Tinsukia, and Tezpur.

Guwahati, the largest city in Northeast India, has witnessed significant real estate appreciation over the past decade. Areas like Uzanbazar, Christian Basti, Beltola, Zoo Road, and the newly developed localities on the outskirts have seen substantial construction activity. The Guwahati Metropolitan Development Authority oversees urban planning and building regulations in the region. The completion of the Bogibeel Bridge and improvements to National Highways have improved connectivity, opening up new areas for residential development.

Assam's real estate market is characterised by a mix of independent houses, apartment complexes, and commercial properties. The state government's housing schemes and PM Awas Yojana have contributed to affordable housing development. Land prices in prime Guwahati localities can rival those in metropolitan cities, though prices in smaller towns remain significantly more reasonable.

How Stamp Duty is Calculated in Assam

Stamp duty in Assam is calculated based on the higher of the agreement value or the circle rate (market value guideline) determined by the state registration department. The department has divided the state into various circles with distinct valuation schedules. The valuation is typically done on a per-square-foot or per-square-metre basis depending on the location and type of property.

The state offers a 1% concession on stamp duty for female buyers. Male buyers pay 5% stamp duty, while female buyers pay 4%. Joint ownership rates vary: both female joint owners pay 4%, both male joint owners pay 5%, and a male-female joint ownership attracts 4.5% stamp duty. Registration charges are uniformly 1% with no upper cap.

Assam also charges a small fee for the transfer of property in urban areas under certain municipal jurisdictions. Buyers should also budget for professional fees including lawyer charges, document writer fees, and the cost of obtaining encumbrance and other certificates.

Property Registration Process in Assam

The registration process in Assam is managed through the Revenue and Disaster Management Department's registration system. Here are the key steps:

  • Step 1: Engage a legal practitioner to draft the sale deed and verify the title of the property.
  • Step 2: Pay the stamp duty either through e-stamping or by purchasing non-judicial stamp paper from authorised vendors. The payment can also be made at designated bank branches.
  • Step 3: Book an appointment online at the concerned Sub-Registrar office. The online portal provides availability slots.
  • Step 4: Present the deed at the Sub-Registrar office. Both parties must be present with original documents and two witnesses. Thumb impressions and photographs are taken during the process.
  • Step 5: After verification and approval by the Sub-Registrar, the deed is registered and the registered copy is returned to the buyer.

Documents Required

  • Duly stamped sale deed
  • Chain of title documents for the last 12-30 years
  • Encumbrance certificate
  • Mutation certificate and land revenue receipts
  • Up-to-date property tax receipts
  • Aadhaar card and PAN card
  • Passport-size photographs
  • Identity proof of two witnesses

Circle rates in Guwahati are determined ward-wise by the district administration and are revised annually or biennially. The rates vary significantly by locality, with prime areas like Uzanbazar and GNB Road having higher rates compared to peripheral areas.
Yes, e-stamping is available in Assam and is increasingly becoming the preferred method for stamp duty payment. The Stock Holding Corporation of India Ltd (SHCIL) manages the e-stamping facility in the state.
