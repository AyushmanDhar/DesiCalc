Overview of Real Estate in Chhattisgarh

Chhattisgarh, carved out of Madhya Pradesh in 2000, has developed a distinct real estate identity centred around its capital Raipur and the industrial city of Bhilai. The state's abundant natural resources and industrial growth, particularly in steel, power, and mining, have driven economic development and, by extension, property demand. Raipur has emerged as a modern and well-planned city with wide roads, organised sectors, and contemporary residential complexes. Other significant real estate markets include Bilaspur, Durg, Korba, Rajnandgaon, and Ambikapur.

Raipur's real estate market has seen steady appreciation, particularly in areas like VIP Road, Shankar Nagar, Devendra Nagar, and the newly developed Purani Basti and Tatibandh regions. The Nava Raipur Atal Nagar project, a greenfield smart city development, is one of India's most ambitious planned city initiatives. This 80,000-hectare development is attracting significant investment in both residential and commercial real estate. The presence of the New Raipur railway station and the Swami Vivekananda Airport has further boosted connectivity and property values.

Bhilai, home to the Bhilai Steel Plant, has a stable real estate market driven by the employment base of the steel plant and related industries. Bilaspur, another major city, has seen growth due to its function as a railway division headquarters and the presence of educational institutions. The state government's focus on improving road connectivity, including the Raipur-Visakhapatnam corridor, is positively impacting real estate markets across the state.

How Stamp Duty is Calculated in Chhattisgarh

Stamp duty in Chhattisgarh is calculated on the higher of the transaction value or the circle rate (ready reckoner rate) prescribed by the state government. The state offers a 1% concession for female buyers, with male buyers paying 5% stamp duty and female buyers paying 4%. Joint ownership rates are: both female owners pay 4%, both male owners pay 5%, and mixed-gender joint ownership attracts 4.5%. Registration charges are uniform at 1% with no upper cap.

Chhattisgarh also levies a transfer duty (stamp duty surcharge) in certain municipal areas. The state has a well-defined circle rate structure that varies by city, zone, and locality. For instance, properties in prime areas of Raipur like Devendra Nagar and Shankar Nagar have higher circle rates compared to peripheral areas. The rates are typically expressed on a per-square-metre basis and are revised periodically by the state government.

Property Registration Process in Chhattisgarh

The registration process in Chhattisgarh is managed by the Department of Commercial Taxes (Registration) through the IGRS Chhattisgarh portal. Here are the key steps:

  • Step 1: Prepare the sale deed with the assistance of a registered document writer. The draft is submitted to the Sub-Registrar office for approval.
  • Step 2: Pay stamp duty through e-stamping at authorised banks or through the online portal. The e-stamp certificate serves as proof of payment.
  • Step 3: Schedule an appointment at the Sub-Registrar office through the online portal. The portal shows available dates and slots.
  • Step 4: Both parties appear before the Sub-Registrar with original documents and witnesses. Biometric verification and photography are completed.
  • Step 5: The Sub-Registrar verifies the deed, payments, and documents. Upon satisfaction, the deed is registered and a copy is provided.

Documents Required

  • Sale deed on e-stamp or stamp paper
  • Previous title documents
  • Encumbrance certificate
  • Mutation certificate and land revenue receipts
  • Property tax receipts
  • Aadhaar card and PAN card
  • Passport-size photographs
  • Witness identity proof

FAQs

Yes, women in Chhattisgarh pay 4% stamp duty compared to 5% for men. The benefit extends to joint ownership when all owners are women, and the mixed-gender joint ownership rate of 4.5% also reflects the concession.
Nava Raipur Atal Nagar has its own circle rate schedule determined by the Nava Raipur Development Authority. Rates vary by sector, with premium sectors commanding higher rates. Being a planned city, the layout and zoning significantly influence property valuation.
Beyond stamp duty and registration charges, buyers may need to pay transfer duty in municipal areas, GST on under-construction properties, and professional fees for legal and documentation services.
E-stamping is the preferred method for stamp duty payment in Chhattisgarh and is widely accepted across all Sub-Registrar offices. Traditional stamp paper is being phased out. E-stamp certificates can be obtained from authorised banks like SBI, PNB, and Axis Bank.
Property ownership can be verified by obtaining an encumbrance certificate from the Sub-Registrar office, checking mutation records at the tehsil office, and reviewing the chain of sale deeds. Online verification through the IGRS portal is also available for registered documents.
