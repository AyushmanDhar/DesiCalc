Overview of Real Estate in Andhra Pradesh

Andhra Pradesh has emerged as one of India's most promising real estate destinations following the bifurcation and the development of Amaravati as its new capital city. The state government's focus on infrastructure development, including the Visakhapatnam-Chennai Industrial Corridor and the Bhogapuram International Airport, has significantly boosted property values across major cities.

Visakhapatnam remains the largest real estate market in Andhra Pradesh, with demand spanning from affordable housing in areas like Pendurthi and Gajuwaka to premium properties in Beach Road and MVP Colony. Amaravati, the greenfield capital city, has witnessed substantial development with government buildings, residential towers, and commercial complexes coming up rapidly. Other cities like Guntur, Kakinada, Rajahmundry, and Tirupati have also seen steady appreciation in property prices due to improved connectivity and local economic growth.

Circle rates in Andhra Pradesh are revised periodically by the state government's registration department. These rates vary by municipality, village, and even specific localities within cities. The department maintains a comprehensive schedule of circle rates that serves as the minimum valuation for property transactions.

How Stamp Duty is Calculated in Andhra Pradesh

Stamp duty in Andhra Pradesh is calculated on the higher of two values: the agreement value (actual sale consideration) or the circle rate (ready reckoner rate) as determined by the state government. This ensures that the government collects appropriate revenue on property transactions. The calculation method depends on the gender composition of the buyers.

For individual male buyers, the stamp duty rate is 5% of the property value. Female buyers benefit from a 1% concession, paying only 4% stamp duty. This incentive is part of the government's policy to promote women's property ownership. Joint ownership scenarios follow a tiered structure: if both buyers are female, the rate is 4%; if both are male, it is 5%; and for a joint ownership with one male and one female buyer, the applicable rate is 4.5%.

Registration charges are levied uniformly at 1% of the property value with no upper cap. The total cost to the buyer, therefore, ranges from 5% to 6% of the property value depending on the buyer category.

Property Registration Process in Andhra Pradesh

Registering a property in Andhra Pradesh involves a straightforward process through the CARD (Computer-aided Administration of Registration Department) system. Here are the step-by-step procedures:

  • Step 1: Draft the sale deed agreement with the help of a registered document writer or advocate. Ensure all property details, buyer and seller information, and consideration amount are correctly mentioned.
  • Step 2: Pay the applicable stamp duty and registration charges. This can be done online through the CARD portal or at designated banks authorised by the registration department.
  • Step 3: Book an appointment at the Sub-Registrar Office having jurisdiction over the property location. The online slot booking system allows you to choose a convenient date and time.
  • Step 4: Visit the Sub-Registrar Office on the appointed date with all original documents. Both buyer and seller must be present along with two witnesses. Biometric verification and photographs will be taken.
  • Step 5: After verification, the deed is registered and a unique document number is generated. The registered deed can be collected the same day or within a few working days.

Documents Required

  • Sale deed agreement duly stamped
  • Previous title documents and chain of ownership
  • Encumbrance certificate (Form 15)
  • Property tax receipts
  • PAN card of both buyer and seller
  • Aadhaar card for identity verification
  • Passport-size photographs (2 copies each)
  • Two witnesses with their ID proof
  • Khata certificate and extracts from municipal authorities
