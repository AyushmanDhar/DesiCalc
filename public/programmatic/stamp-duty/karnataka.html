FAQs

Women in Karnataka pay the same 5% stamp duty rate as men, as MP charges a flat rate for all buyers.
The total cost of property registration in Karnataka is 6% of the property value — stamp duty 5% + registry charges 1% (+10% cess). Additional costs include document writer fees, encumbrance certificate, and legal fees.
Yes, e-stamping is widely available and is the preferred method for stamp duty payment in Karnataka. E-stamp certificates can be obtained from authorised banks including SBI, PNB, and Axis Bank.
Besides stamp duty and registry charges, buyers should budget for legal fees, document writer charges, encumbrance certificate fees, mutation charges, and GST on under-construction properties. These typically add 1-2% to the total cost.
