Overview of Real Estate in Arunachal Pradesh

Arunachal Pradesh, India's northeasternmost state, has a unique real estate landscape shaped by its topography, cultural diversity, and special regulations under Article 371(A) of the Constitution. The state's property market is primarily concentrated in its urban centres like Itanagar, Naharlagun, Pasighat, Tawang, and Ziro. Given the hilly terrain, land availability is limited, which naturally constrains supply and influences property pricing.

The real estate market in Arunachal Pradesh is distinct because of the Inner Line Permit (ILP) system, which regulates the entry of outsiders. Property ownership is largely restricted to state subjects, and non-Arunachalee individuals face significant legal barriers when attempting to purchase land. This framework has kept the market insular and relatively less volatile compared to other Indian states. Most real estate activity involves local residents and government employees posted in the state.

Itanagar, the capital city, has witnessed steady demand for both residential and commercial properties. Government housing schemes and infrastructure projects have been the primary drivers of construction activity. The state government's focus on improving road connectivity through projects like the Trans-Arunachal Highway has started opening up new areas for development.

How Stamp Duty is Calculated in Arunachal Pradesh

Arunachal Pradesh follows a uniform stamp duty structure with no gender-based differentiation. The stamp duty rate is a flat 6% applicable to all categories of buyers, regardless of gender or joint ownership structure. This makes it one of the simpler stamp duty regimes in India from a calculation perspective.

Registration charges are levied at 1% of the property value with no upper cap, bringing the total transaction cost to 7% of the property value for all buyer categories. There are no concessions for female buyers, no surcharges, and no additional cess applicable in the state. The calculation is straightforward: the applicable rate is applied to the higher of the agreement value or the circle rate as determined by the state registration department.

Circle rates in Arunachal Pradesh vary by location and are revised periodically. Buyers should check the latest ready reckoner rates from the district Sub-Registrar offices as online availability of circle rate data for Arunachal Pradesh is more limited compared to other states.

Property Registration Process in Arunachal Pradesh

The property registration process in Arunachal Pradesh involves the following steps:

  • Step 1: Prepare the sale deed with the assistance of a legal professional. Ensure compliance with the Arunachal Pradesh Land Holding and Transfer Regulation, 1977.
  • Step 2: Pay the stamp duty at the designated treasury or through authorised stamp vendors. E-stamping is available in limited locations.
  • Step 3: Submit the deed along with all supporting documents at the Sub-Registrar office having jurisdiction over the property.
  • Step 4: Both parties must appear in person at the registration office along with two witnesses for verification and biometric data capture.
  • Step 5: After verification by the Sub-Registrar, the deed is registered and a copy is provided to the parties.

Documents Required

  • Original sale deed
  • Proof of identity (Aadhaar, Voter ID, Passport)
  • Proof of Arunachal Pradesh state subject certificate
  • Land passbook or landholding certificate
  • Previous title documents
  • Encumbrance certificate
  • Tax receipts for the property
  • Two passport-size photographs
  • Two witnesses with valid ID proof
