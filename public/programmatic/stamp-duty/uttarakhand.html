FAQs

Women in Uttarakhand pay 5% stamp duty, which is 1% lower than the 6% rate for men. This concession is available for individual female buyers and joint ownership where all owners are women.
The total cost of property registration in Uttarakhand includes stamp duty (6% for men, 5% for women) plus 1% registry charges. Additional costs include document writer fees, encumbrance certificate, and legal fees.
Yes, e-stamping is widely available and is the preferred method for stamp duty payment in Uttarakhand. E-stamp certificates can be obtained from authorised banks including SBI, PNB, and Axis Bank.
Besides stamp duty and registry charges, buyers should budget for legal fees, document writer charges, encumbrance certificate fees, mutation charges, and GST on under-construction properties. These typically add 1-2% to the total cost.
