Vehicle Registration Process in Madhya Pradesh

Madhya Pradesh vehicle registration is managed by the MP Transport Department with RTO offices across all districts. Major RTOs include Bhopal (MP-04), Indore (MP-09), Jabalpur (MP-20), Gwalior (MP-07), Ujjain (MP-13), Sagar (MP-15), Rewa (MP-17), Guna (MP-08), Chhindwara (MP-17), and Ratlam (MP-12). The state follows the Central Motor Vehicle Rules with taxation under the MP Motor Vehicles Taxation Act.

One unique aspect of Madhya Pradesh is its quarterly tax payment model. Unlike most states where road tax is paid as a one-time lump sum at registration, MP offers a quarterly payment option. This can be advantageous for buyers who prefer lower upfront costs, though the total payment over the vehicle's life may be higher due to periodic collection charges. The registration process otherwise follows the standard VAHAN framework with dealer-assisted temporary registration followed by permanent registration at the RTO.

Road Tax Calculation in Madhya Pradesh

Madhya Pradesh uses a slab-based road tax system with different rates for petrol and diesel vehicles. Petrol and CNG vehicles are taxed at 8% for vehicles up to &₹;10 lakh, 10% for &₹;10-20 lakh, and 14% for above &₹;20 lakh. Diesel vehicles face higher rates of 10%, 12%, and 16% respectively across the same slabs.

For example, an &₹;8 lakh petrol car in MP costs &₹;64,000 in road tax (8%). A &₹;12 lakh diesel SUV costs &₹;1,44,000 (12%). A &₹;30 lakh diesel luxury car attracts 16% tax (&₹;4,80,000). The quarterly payment model means you can pay these amounts in instalments rather than upfront, though most dealers and buyers still prefer the one-time payment option at registration.

EV Policy in Madhya Pradesh

Madhya Pradesh does not offer any special road tax exemption for electric vehicles. EVs are taxed at the standard rates applicable to petrol vehicles of the same price range. This makes MP one of the few states without EV tax incentives. However, the central government's FAME II subsidies still apply to EV purchases in MP. The state has been slower in adopting EV-friendly policies compared to other states.

Quarterly Tax Model

MP's unique quarterly tax system allows vehicle owners to pay road tax in quarterly instalments rather than a lump sum. While this reduces the upfront cost at registration, the total tax over 15 years can be higher due to administrative charges. Most buyers opt for the one-time payment option available at the time of registration, which offers a slight discount compared to the cumulative quarterly payments.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the RTO code for Madhya Pradesh?

Madhya Pradesh uses the MP prefix. Major codes include MP-01 (Bhopal Rural), MP-02 (Bhopal Urban), MP-03 (Bhopal), MP-04 (Bhopal Northern), MP-05 (Bhopal), MP-06 (Bina), MP-07 (Gwalior), MP-08 (Guna), MP-09 (Indore), MP-10 (Indore West), MP-11 (Khandwa), MP-12 (Ratlam), MP-13 (Ujjain), MP-14 (Mandsaur), MP-15 (Sagar), MP-16 (Damoh), MP-17 (Rewa), MP-18 (Satna), MP-19 (Shahdol), and MP-20 (Jabalpur).

How much is road tax for a &₹;10 lakh car in MP?

For a &₹;10 lakh petrol car in MP, road tax is 8% (&₹;80,000). For a diesel car, it is 10% (&₹;1,00,000). Registration (&₹;600) and HSRP (&₹;400) are additional.

Does MP have a quarterly tax system?

Yes, MP offers a quarterly tax payment option though the one-time lump sum payment is also available and more commonly used. The quarterly option can be advantageous for those who want to spread out the cost.

Are EVs exempt from road tax in Madhya Pradesh?

No, Madhya Pradesh does not offer any road tax exemption for EVs. They are taxed at the same rates as petrol vehicles. This is uncommon among Indian states, most of which offer at least partial EV tax benefits.

What is the road tax for used vehicle transfer in MP?

For transferring a used vehicle to MP from another state, the road tax is calculated on the depreciated value using the applicable slab rates. The quarterly option is also available for used vehicle tax payments.

Is HSRP mandatory in Madhya Pradesh?

Yes, HSRP is mandatory for all new vehicle registrations in MP. The cost is &₹;400 for four-wheelers and &₹;200 for two-wheelers. These plates are available at authorised HSRP centres across the state.
