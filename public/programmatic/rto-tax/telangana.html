Vehicle Registration Process in Telangana

Telangana, India's youngest state formed in 2014, has a modern and progressive vehicle registration system managed by the Telangana Transport Department — notably called the Road Transport Authority (RTA) rather than RTO, a terminology difference from most other states. The major RTA offices include Hyderabad (TS 08, TS 09, TS 10), Cyberabad (TS 07), Warangal (TS 03), Nizamabad (TS 22), Khammam (TS 04), Karimnagar (TS 21), and Mahabubnagar (TS 19). The Hyderabad and Cyberabad RTAs together handle over 60% of all new vehicle registrations in the state, reflecting the concentration of population and economic activity in the Greater Hyderabad region.

Telangana's vehicle registration system is fully digitised through the TSTS (Telangana State Transport) portal, which integrates with the national Vahan and Sarathi platforms. The state's Transport Department has been recognised for its efficient online services, including doorstep registration for select vehicle categories. Hyderabad's rapid urban expansion has led to a surge in vehicle registrations, with the city consistently ranking among the top three in India for new car sales. The state also has a significant two-wheeler market, particularly in semi-urban and rural areas where two-wheelers are the primary mode of personal transport.

How Road Tax Is Calculated in Telangana

Telangana employs a progressive slab-based road tax system, similar to Tamil Nadu but with slightly different rates. Vehicles priced up to ₹5,00,000 are taxed at 13%. The rate increases to 14% for vehicles between ₹5,00,001 and ₹10,00,000, 17% for vehicles between ₹10,00,001 and ₹20,00,000, and 18% for vehicles above ₹20,00,000. Additionally, Telangana imposes a unique Road Safety Cess of ₹5,000 on all new vehicle registrations, regardless of the vehicle type or price. This cess is used to fund road safety infrastructure and awareness programs across the state.

Consider a car priced at ₹8,00,000 in Telangana. It falls in the 14% slab: road tax = ₹8,00,000 × 14% = ₹1,12,000. Add registration ₹600, HSRP ₹400, and Road Safety Cess ₹5,000, making the total RTO cost ₹1,18,000. A luxury car at ₹25,00,000 in the 18% slab would incur ₹25,00,000 × 18% = ₹4,50,000 in road tax, plus ₹600 + ₹400 + ₹5,000, totalling ₹4,56,000. An electric vehicle at any price would have 0% road tax but still pay the ₹5,000 cess: total RTO cost ₹5,000 + ₹600 + ₹400 = ₹6,000. The Road Safety Cess is a fixed cost that affects all buyers equally and is a distinctive feature of Telangana's tax structure.

Electric Vehicle (EV) Exemption Policy

Telangana offers a full road tax exemption for electric vehicles, though the Road Safety Cess of ₹5,000 still applies to EV registrations. The EV exemption covers all categories of battery electric vehicles. The state has also launched the Telangana EV Policy 2025-30, which includes additional incentives such as charging infrastructure subsidies and parking fee waivers for EVs. Hyderabad has emerged as a major EV manufacturing hub, with several companies setting up production facilities in the state, making it an EV-friendly ecosystem for both manufacturers and consumers.

Required Documents for Vehicle Registration

For vehicle registration in Telangana, the following documents are required: Form 20 (application), Form 21 (dealer invoice), Form 22 (manufacturer's certificate), valid insurance policy, address and identity proof, PAN card, passport-size photographs, and the temporary registration certificate. For vehicles financed through banks or NBFCs, the hypothecation agreement (Form 34) must be submitted. All documents must be self-attested and submitted online through the TSTS portal or in person at the RTA office.
