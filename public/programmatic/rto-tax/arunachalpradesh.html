Vehicle Registration Process in Arunachal Pradesh

Arunachal Pradesh vehicle registration is managed by the State Transport Department with RTO offices across districts like Itanagar, Naharlagun, Pasighat, Tawang, and Ziro. The registration process follows the Central Motor Vehicle Rules but with state-specific tax rates. Buyers must complete registration within 30 days of vehicle purchase.

The dealer typically handles temporary registration and provides Form 20 and Form 21. You then visit the local RTO for permanent registration. Given the hilly terrain, vehicle roadworthiness checks are particularly important in Arunachal Pradesh. The RTO issues a registration number in the AR-XX format.

Required Documents

  • Form 20 (Application for Vehicle Registration)
  • Form 21 (Certificate of Sale)
  • Form 22 (Manufacturer's Roadworthiness Certificate)
  • Valid Motor Insurance Policy
  • Address Proof (Aadhaar, Voter ID, or State-specific residence proof)
  • Identity Proof (Aadhaar, PAN, Passport)
  • Invoice from the vehicle dealer
  • Pollution Under Control (PUC) Certificate
  • HSRP order receipt

Road Tax Calculation in Arunachal Pradesh

Arunachal Pradesh uses a flat-rate road tax system, making it one of the more affordable states for vehicle registration. Petrol cars are charged a flat 5% of the ex-showroom price, diesel cars at 6%, and CNG vehicles at 5%. The absence of slab-based taxation simplifies calculation significantly compared to neighbouring states.

For example, a ₹10 lakh petrol car in Arunachal Pradesh attracts road tax of ₹50,000 (5%). A diesel SUV priced at ₹15 lakh would cost ₹90,000 in road tax (6%). The low tax rates make Arunachal Pradesh attractive for vehicle buyers, especially those purchasing higher-value vehicles.

EV Policy in Arunachal Pradesh

Arunachal Pradesh offers a full road tax exemption for electric vehicles as part of the central government's FAME II scheme adoption. EV buyers pay zero road tax on their first registration. The state has been promoting EVs to reduce dependence on fossil fuels in its ecologically sensitive Himalayan region. Charging infrastructure is gradually expanding in Itanagar and other district headquarters.

Two-Wheeler Registration

Two-wheelers in Arunachal Pradesh are taxed at 4% of the ex-showroom price, with a registration fee of ₹300 and HSRP charge of ₹200. This low rate makes bike ownership affordable in the state, which is important given that two-wheelers are the most common mode of personal transport in the hilly terrain.
