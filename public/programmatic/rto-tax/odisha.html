Vehicle Registration Process in Odisha

Odisha, the land of Lord Jagannath, has a comprehensive vehicle registration system managed by the Odisha Transport Department. The state is divided into several RTO zones including Bhubaneswar (OD 02), Cuttack (OD 05), Puri (OD 13), Berhampur (OD 07), Sambalpur (OD 15), Rourkela (OD 14), Balasore (OD 01), and Jeypore (OD 17), among others. The Bhubaneswar RTO, being the capital city office, handles the highest volume of vehicle registrations in the state. Odisha also has a unique RTO code system where each district is assigned a two-digit code prefixed with OD.

Odisha's economy is a mix of agriculture, mining, and tourism, which influences the vehicle registration landscape. The mining districts of Keonjhar, Sundargarh, and Jharsuguda see high volumes of commercial vehicle registrations, including heavy goods carriers and mining trucks. The coastal districts of Puri and Ganjam have a significant number of tourist vehicle registrations. The state also has a growing two-wheeler market in its semi-urban and rural areas. The Odisha Transport Department has been actively digitising its services through the e-Transport portal, enabling online fee payments, application tracking, and appointment scheduling for vehicle inspections.

How Road Tax Is Calculated in Odisha

Odisha uses a flat-rate road tax system for private vehicles. Petrol and CNG vehicles attract a 5% tax on their ex-showroom price, while diesel vehicles are taxed at 6%. Electric vehicles are fully exempt from road tax. Commercial vehicles, including goods carriers, taxis, and buses, are taxed at 6% of the ex-showroom price. The flat-rate system is one of the simplest in the country, with no price slabs or brackets to navigate.

For example, a buyer purchasing a petrol car at ₹9,50,000 in Odisha would pay a road tax of ₹9,50,000 × 5% = ₹47,500. The registration fee is a flat ₹600, and HSRP installation costs ₹400, making the total RTO expenditure ₹48,500. If the same car were a diesel variant, the tax would be ₹9,50,000 × 6% = ₹57,000, totalling ₹58,000 including registration and HSRP. An electric car priced at ₹9,50,000 would incur just ₹1,000 in RTO charges — a saving of ₹47,500 compared to the petrol version.

Electric Vehicle (EV) Exemption Policy

Odisha has adopted a full road tax exemption policy for all categories of electric vehicles as part of its commitment to the FAME II scheme and the national EV mission. The exemption covers battery electric vehicles (BEVs) across two-wheelers, three-wheelers, and four-wheelers. There is no cap on the vehicle price or any expiry date currently announced for this exemption. This makes Odisha one of the more attractive states for EV buyers, especially in cities like Bhubaneswar and Cuttack where the charging infrastructure is gradually expanding.

Required Documents for Vehicle Registration

When registering a vehicle in Odisha, you need: Form 20 (duly filled registration application), Form 21 (dealer sale certificate), Form 22 (manufacturer's road worthiness certificate), valid insurance policy (at least third-party), address proof (Aadhaar, Voter ID, or driving licence), identity proof, PAN card, and passport-size photographs. For commercial vehicles, additional documents include permit applications, fitness certificates, and route permits where applicable.
