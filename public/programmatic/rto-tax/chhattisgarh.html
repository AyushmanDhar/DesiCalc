Vehicle Registration Process in Chhattisgarh

Chhattisgarh's vehicle registration is managed by the Chhattisgarh Transport Department with RTO offices in all districts. Major RTOs are in Raipur (CG-07), Bhilai (CG-04), Bilaspur (CG-10), Durg (CG-09), Rajnandgaon (CG-08), and Korba (CG-11). The state follows the Central Motor Vehicle Rules with state-specific taxation under the Chhattisgarh Motor Vehicles Taxation Act.

The registration process begins at the dealership where you purchase the vehicle. The dealer provides temporary registration and submits Form 20 electronically through the VAHAN portal. You then visit the RTO for physical verification of the vehicle. The RTO officer checks the engine number, chassis number, and confirms the vehicle specifications. Upon successful verification, a registration number is allotted in the CG-XX-XXXX format.

Required Documents for Registration

  • Form 20 (Vehicle Registration Application)
  • Form 21 (Dealer Sale Certificate)
  • Form 22 (Roadworthiness Certificate from Manufacturer)
  • Insurance Certificate (third-party or comprehensive)
  • Address Proof (Aadhaar, Voter ID, Electricity Bill, Rent Agreement)
  • Identity Proof (Aadhaar, PAN, Driving Licence)
  • Original Invoice and Delivery Note
  • Pollution Under Control Certificate
  • HSRP receipt
  • Passport-size photographs

Road Tax Calculation in Chhattisgarh

Chhattisgarh offers some of the lowest road tax rates in India with a flat-rate system. Petrol and CNG cars are taxed at just 4% of the ex-showroom price, while diesel cars are taxed at 5%. This makes Chhattisgarh one of the most affordable states for vehicle registration, comparable only to a few other states with similarly low rates.

For example, a &₹;10 lakh petrol car in Chhattisgarh attracts only &₹;40,000 in road tax. A &₹;20 lakh diesel SUV would cost &₹;1,00,000 in tax. Compare this to neighbouring Madhya Pradesh where the same car could attract 10% tax for diesel models. For high-value vehicles, the savings in Chhattisgarh can be substantial, often exceeding &₹;1-2 lakh compared to slab-based states.

EV Exemption in Chhattisgarh

Chhattisgarh offers 100% road tax exemption for electric vehicles under its state EV policy. The exemption applies to battery electric vehicles across all categories — cars, two-wheelers, three-wheelers, and commercial EVs. The state government has been actively promoting EV adoption in cities like Raipur and Bilaspur, with plans to set up charging stations at government buildings and public spaces.

Two-Wheeler Taxation

Two-wheelers in Chhattisgarh are taxed at the minimal rate of 3% of ex-showroom price, with registration fee of &₹;300 and HSRP of &₹;200. For a commuter bike priced at &₹;1 lakh, the total registration cost is approximately &₹;3,500. These low rates make Chhattisgarh an attractive state for two-wheeler buyers, encouraging personal mobility in both urban and rural areas.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the RTO code for Chhattisgarh?

Chhattisgarh uses the CG prefix. Key codes include CG-04 (Durg-Bhilai), CG-06 (Raigarh), CG-07 (Raipur East), CG-08 (Rajnandgaon), CG-09 (Durg), CG-10 (Bilaspur), CG-11 (Korba), CG-12 (Raipur West), and CG-13 (Ambikapur).

How much is road tax for a &₹;10 lakh car in Chhattisgarh?

For a &₹;10 lakh petrol car, road tax is &₹;40,000 (4%). For a diesel car, it is &₹;50,000 (5%). Registration fee of &₹;600 and HSRP of &₹;400 are additional.

Does Chhattisgarh have the lowest road tax in India?

Chhattisgarh has among the lowest road tax rates in India at 4% for petrol cars. Only a few states like Arunachal Pradesh (5%) and Goa (4%) have comparable or lower rates. However, some states have even lower rates for specific categories.

Are EVs fully exempt in Chhattisgarh?

Yes, electric vehicles are fully exempt from road tax in Chhattisgarh. The exemption covers all categories of EVs for first registration.

What is the process for re-registration of a used vehicle in CG?

For transferring a used vehicle to Chhattisgarh from another state, you need to obtain NOC (Form 28) from the original RTO, submit Form 27 for transfer, and apply for fresh registration at your local CG RTO. Road tax is recalculated on the depreciated value.

Is HSRP mandatory in Chhattisgarh?

Yes, HSRP is mandatory for all vehicles registered in Chhattisgarh as per Supreme Court directives. The charge is &₹;400 for four-wheelers and &₹;200 for two-wheelers. These plates come with a laser-etched code that helps in vehicle identification and theft prevention.
