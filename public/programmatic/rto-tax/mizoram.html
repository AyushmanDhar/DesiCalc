Vehicle Registration Process in Mizoram

Mizoram, the southernmost state of Northeast India, administers its vehicle registration through the Mizoram Transport Department with Regional Transport Offices in Aizawl (MZ 01), Lunglei (MZ 02), Champhai (MZ 03), Serchhip (MZ 04), Kolasib (MZ 06), Lawngtlai (MZ 07), Mamit (MZ 08), and Siaha (MZ 09). The Aizawl RTO is the busiest office, handling registrations for the capital city and surrounding areas. The hilly terrain of Mizoram, with its steep roads and sharp curves, makes vehicle registration a critical process ensuring that all vehicles meet the necessary road worthiness standards.

Two-wheelers and compact SUVs are the most popular vehicle categories in Mizoram due to the narrow, winding roads that characterise most of the state's urban and rural areas. The vehicle registration process in Mizoram has been partially digitised through the Vahan and Sarathi portals, enabling applicants to book appointments, submit forms, and make payments online. However, the physical inspection of the vehicle by a Motor Vehicle Inspector remains a mandatory in-person step. The state also enforces strict emission norms given the ecological sensitivity of its location within the Indo-Burma biodiversity hotspot.

How Road Tax Is Calculated in Mizoram

Mizoram employs a straightforward flat-rate road tax structure. Petrol vehicles are taxed at 5% of their ex-showroom price, while diesel vehicles attract a higher rate of 6%. CNG vehicles are treated on par with petrol vehicles at 5%. Electric vehicles receive a full tax exemption at 0% as part of the state's commitment to sustainable mobility. Commercial vehicles, including buses and goods carriers, are taxed at 6%. This flat-rate system makes it simple for buyers to calculate their total RTO outlay without needing to factor in price brackets or slab thresholds.

Consider a petrol hatchback with an ex-showroom price of ₹8,00,000. The road tax in Mizoram would be ₹8,00,000 × 5% = ₹40,000. Adding the registration fee of ₹600 and HSRP cost of ₹400 brings the total RTO charges to ₹41,000. For a diesel SUV priced at ₹18,00,000, the road tax would be ₹18,00,000 × 6% = ₹1,08,000, plus ₹600 and ₹400, totalling ₹1,09,000. An equivalent EV with an ex-showroom price of ₹18,00,000 would cost only ₹1,000 in RTO charges, highlighting the significant savings available to EV buyers.

Electric Vehicle (EV) Exemption Policy

Mizoram provides a full road tax exemption for all categories of electric vehicles. This includes electric two-wheelers, three-wheelers, and four-wheelers. The exemption is unconditional — there is no cap on the vehicle price or any time limit currently imposed. This policy aligns with the FAME India scheme and the larger national goal of achieving 30% EV penetration by 2030. Mizoram's EV policy is particularly significant given the state's commitment to preserving its pristine natural environment and reducing vehicular emissions in its hill towns.

Required Documents for Vehicle Registration

The document checklist for vehicle registration in Mizoram includes: Form 20 completed and signed, Form 21 (sale certificate from the authorised dealer), Form 22 (manufacturer's road worthiness certificate), valid motor insurance policy, address proof (Aadhaar, electricity bill, or bank statement), identity proof (Voter ID, Passport, or Driving Licence), PAN card, and two recent passport-size photographs. All original documents must be presented along with self-attested photocopies at the RTO counter.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I drive a vehicle with temporary registration in Mizoram? A: Yes, temporary registration is valid for one month, during which you can operate the vehicle within Mizoram and neighbouring states.

Q: What happens if I do not register my vehicle within the prescribed time? A: Late registration attracts a penalty that can range from ₹500 to ₹5,000 depending on the delay period.

Q: Is there any special tax for tourist vehicles in Mizoram? A: Tourist vehicles are treated as commercial vehicles and taxed at the standard commercial rate of 6%.

Q: Can I transfer my existing vehicle registration from another state to Mizoram? A: Yes, you can apply for re-registration in Mizoram by submitting Form 27 and Form 28 along with the No Objection Certificate from the original RTO.

Q: Does Mizoram have a green tax for older vehicles? A: As of 2026, Mizoram has not implemented a separate green tax for older vehicles, but all vehicles must pass the Pollution Under Control (PUC) test annually.
