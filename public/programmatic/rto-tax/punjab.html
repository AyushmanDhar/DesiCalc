Vehicle Registration Process in Punjab

Punjab, the land of five rivers, has a well-established vehicle registration framework under the Punjab Transport Department. The state's RTO network covers all major cities including Chandigarh (CH 01 — shared with Haryana), Ludhiana (PB 10), Jalandhar (PB 08), Amritsar (PB 02), Patiala (PB 11), Bathinda (PB 03), Mohali (PB 65), and Fatehgarh Sahib (PB 23). The Ludhiana RTO processes the highest number of vehicle registrations in the state, driven by the city's status as Punjab's industrial and manufacturing hub.

Punjab's agricultural economy heavily influences its vehicle registration profile. Tractors and farm equipment form a significant portion of registrations, particularly in the Malwa region districts of Sangrur, Barnala, Mansa, and Bathinda. The state also has a high density of private cars and two-wheelers, with Ludhiana and Chandigarh leading in per-capita vehicle ownership. Punjab's proximity to Delhi and the NCR region means a substantial number of vehicles are registered by NRIs and non-resident Punjabis who maintain vehicles in the state. The registration process has been streamlined with the Vahan online platform, but physical inspection of vehicles at the RTO remains a mandatory requirement.

How Road Tax Is Calculated in Punjab

Punjab follows a flat-rate road tax system applicable to all vehicle categories. Petrol and CNG vehicles are taxed at 5% of the ex-showroom price. Diesel vehicles attract a flat 6% rate. Electric vehicles are fully exempt from road tax. Commercial vehicles are also taxed at the standard commercial rate of 6%. There are no price slabs in Punjab's system, making it one of the most straightforward tax structures in North India.

For a popular petrol SUV in Punjab priced at ₹14,00,000, the road tax calculation is: ₹14,00,000 × 5% = ₹70,000. With registration fees of ₹600 and HSRP charges of ₹400, the total RTO cost is ₹71,000. A diesel variant would incur ₹14,00,000 × 6% = ₹84,000 in road tax, plus ₹1,000 for registration and HSRP, totalling ₹85,000. An electric vehicle of the same price would cost just ₹1,000 in RTO charges. These calculations help buyers plan their vehicle purchase budget accurately.

Electric Vehicle (EV) Exemption Policy

Punjab offers a full road tax exemption on electric vehicles in line with the central FAME II scheme. All categories of EVs — two-wheelers, three-wheelers, and four-wheelers — are eligible for zero road tax. The exemption applies to the entire ex-showroom price without any upper limit. Punjab has also been promoting EV adoption through its Punjab Electric Vehicle Policy, which aims to set up charging infrastructure across major cities and highways including the Ludhiana-Delhi and Amritsar-Chandigarh corridors.

Required Documents for Vehicle Registration

The document checklist for Punjab vehicle registration includes: Form 20 (registration application), Form 21 (dealer invoice), Form 22 (manufacturer's certificate), valid insurance certificate, address proof (Aadhaar, driving licence, or utility bill), identity proof, PAN card, and passport-size photographs. NRI applicants may need to provide additional documentation such as a valid passport and proof of NRI status for certain registration categories.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I register a vehicle in Punjab if I live in Chandigarh? A: Chandigarh has a separate registration system (CH series). If your permanent address is in Punjab but you reside in Chandigarh, you must register the vehicle in Punjab.

Q: What is the penalty for not transferring vehicle ownership in Punjab? A: Failure to transfer ownership within 30 days of sale can attract a fine ranging from ₹1,000 to ₹5,000 depending on the delay.

Q: Is there a separate tax for tractors in Punjab? A: Tractors and agricultural machinery are classified separately and may be subject to different tax rates as per the Punjab Motor Vehicles Taxation Act.

Q: Can I get a fancy registration number in Punjab? A: Yes, Punjab offers fancy or VIP registration numbers through an online auction process conducted by the transport department.

Q: Do electric vehicles need to pay any road tax in Punjab? A: No, EVs are fully exempt from road tax in Punjab. Only the nominal registration fee of ₹600 and HSRP charges of ₹400 apply.
