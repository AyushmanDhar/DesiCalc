Vehicle Registration Process in Himachal Pradesh

Vehicle registration in Himachal Pradesh is managed by the HP Transport Department with RTO offices across the state including Shimla (HP-01), Solan (HP-02), Kangra (HP-03), Mandi (HP-04), Kullu (HP-05), Hamirpur (HP-06), Bilaspur (HP-07), and Dharamshala (HP-08). Given the mountainous terrain, vehicle registration in HP involves additional roadworthiness checks to ensure vehicles are safe for hilly roads.

The registration process follows the standard Central Motor Vehicle Rules. New vehicle buyers get temporary registration from the dealer valid for 30 days. Permanent registration requires Form 20 submission at the RTO along with all supporting documents. Given the hilly terrain, the physical vehicle inspection by the RTO officer is particularly thorough, checking brake systems, headlights, and overall condition. Upon approval, a registration number in the HP-XX-XXXX format is issued.

Required Documents for Registration

  • Form 20 (Application for Vehicle Registration)
  • Form 21 (Sale Certificate from Dealer)
  • Form 22 (Roadworthiness Certificate)
  • Valid Insurance Policy (Third-party or Comprehensive)
  • Address Proof (Aadhaar, Voter ID, Passport)
  • Identity Proof (Aadhaar, PAN, Driving Licence)
  • Original Vehicle Invoice and Sale Receipt
  • PUC Certificate
  • HSRP booking receipt
  • Passport-size photographs (2 copies)

Road Tax Calculation in Himachal Pradesh

Himachal Pradesh follows a flat-rate road tax system with very affordable rates. Petrol and CNG cars are taxed at just 4% of the ex-showroom price, while diesel cars are taxed at 5%. There are no slab-based increases or additional cesses, making HP one of the most cost-effective states for vehicle registration in India.

For example, a ₹10 lakh petrol car in Himachal Pradesh attracts only ₹40,000 in road tax. A ₹15 lakh diesel SUV would cost ₹75,000 in tax. These low rates are particularly beneficial for tourists and second-home owners who register vehicles in HP. The state's popularity as a tourist destination means many people choose to register vehicles here, especially for use in hill stations like Shimla, Manali, and Dharamshala.

EV Exemption in Himachal Pradesh

Himachal Pradesh offers full road tax exemption for electric vehicles under the state's clean energy initiatives. The state has been promoting EVs to reduce pollution in its ecologically sensitive Himalayan region. The exemption applies to all EV categories for first registration. HP has also been installing EV charging stations along major tourist routes including the Chandigarh-Shimla and Delhi-Manali corridors.

Two-Wheeler Taxation

Two-wheelers in Himachal Pradesh are taxed at a minimal 3% of ex-showroom price with registration fee of ₹300 and HSRP of ₹200. Two-wheelers are extremely popular in HP's hill towns where narrow roads make them the most practical mode of transport. The low tax rate encourages affordable personal mobility.
