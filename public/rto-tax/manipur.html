Vehicle Registration Process in Manipur

Manipur, the jewel of northeast India, has its vehicle registration system managed by the Manipur Transport Department under the Motor Vehicles Act, 1988. The state has Regional Transport Offices (RTOs) located in Imphal, Bishnupur, Thoubal, Churachandpur, Senapati, Ukhrul, Tamenglong, and Chandel. The Imphal RTO handles the highest volume of registrations given the state capital's concentrated population. Vehicle owners in Manipur must register their vehicles at the RTO within their jurisdiction, typically within seven days of purchase. The process involves submitting Form 20 along with supporting documents, undergoing a vehicle inspection conducted by the Motor Vehicle Inspector (MVI), and paying the applicable road tax and registration fees at the designated treasury or through the online portal.

Manipur's unique geography — encompassing the Imphal valley and surrounding hill districts — influences vehicle preferences significantly. In the valley regions, passenger cars and two-wheelers dominate, while in hill districts like Ukhrul and Tamenglong, SUVs and rugged vehicles are preferred due to challenging road conditions. All new vehicles in Manipur must also be fitted with High-Security Registration Plates (HSRP), which cost ₹400 and are mandatory across the state. The HSRP installation is typically done at the dealer level or at authorised HSRP fitting centres recognised by the state transport department.

How Road Tax Is Calculated in Manipur

Manipur follows a flat-rate road tax structure, meaning the tax is a fixed percentage of the vehicle's ex-showroom price regardless of the vehicle's cost bracket. For petrol and CNG vehicles, the road tax is set at 5% of the ex-showroom price. Diesel vehicles are taxed slightly higher at 6%, reflecting the central government's policy of discouraging diesel usage due to its higher emission levels. Electric vehicles enjoy a full road tax exemption at 0%, making Manipur an attractive destination for EV adoption. Commercial vehicles, including goods carriers and passenger transport vehicles, are taxed at 6% of the ex-showroom price.

To calculate the road tax for a petrol car priced at ₹10,00,000 in Manipur, you simply multiply the ex-showroom price by 5%: ₹10,00,000 × 5% = ₹50,000. Add to this the registration fee of ₹600 and HSRP charges of ₹400, bringing the total RTO charges to ₹51,000. For a diesel vehicle of the same price, the tax would be ₹10,00,000 × 6% = ₹60,000, plus ₹600 registration and ₹400 HSRP, totalling ₹61,000. This straightforward calculation makes it easy for buyers to estimate their on-road price before visiting the dealer.

Electric Vehicle (EV) Exemption Policy

Manipur has aligned with the central government's FAME II scheme by offering a full road tax exemption for electric vehicles. This means EV buyers in Manipur pay 0% road tax on the ex-showroom price of their electric cars, scooters, or bikes. Only the standard registration fee of ₹600 and HSRP charges of ₹400 apply. This exemption is part of a broader push to encourage zero-emission mobility in the state, which is particularly relevant for Manipur's environmentally sensitive hill ecosystems. The EV exemption applies to all categories of electric vehicles, including two-wheelers, three-wheelers, and four-wheelers.

Required Documents for Vehicle Registration

When registering a vehicle in Manipur, the following documents are required: Form 20 (application for registration), Form 21 (sale certificate from the dealer), Form 22 (road worthiness certificate), valid insurance policy, address proof (Aadhaar, Voter ID, Passport, or utility bill), PAN card or Form 60, passport-size photographs, and the temporary registration number (TR) if applicable. All documents must be self-attested and submitted in both original and photocopy form to the concerned RTO.
