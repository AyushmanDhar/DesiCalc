Vehicle Registration Process in Haryana

Haryana's vehicle registration is managed by the Haryana Transport Department with RTO offices across all districts including Gurugram (HR-55), Faridabad (HR-51), Panchkula (HR-03), Ambala (HR-01), Karnal (HR-05), Rohtak (HR-10), Hisar (HR-20), and Sonipat (HR-11). Gurugram and Faridabad, being part of the NCR, have additional regulations aligned with Delhi's pollution control measures.

The registration process in Haryana follows the national VAHAN framework. New vehicle buyers receive temporary registration from the dealer valid for 30 days. Permanent registration requires submitting Form 20 along with supporting documents at the RTO. Haryana RTOs have implemented online appointment systems to reduce waiting times. Upon verification, a registration number in HR-XX-XXXX format is issued. Vehicles in NCR districts must also comply with the colour-coded sticker system similar to Delhi.

Required Documents for Registration

  • Form 20 (duly filled application form)
  • Form 21 (Sale Certificate from authorised dealer)
  • Form 22 (Certificate of Roadworthiness)
  • Insurance Certificate (Third-party or Comprehensive)
  • Address Proof (Aadhaar, Voter ID, Passport, Utility Bill)
  • Identity Proof (Aadhaar, PAN, Driving Licence)
  • Original Invoice and Delivery Challan
  • PUC Certificate
  • HSRP booking receipt
  • Passport-size photographs (2 copies)

How Haryana Road Tax is Calculated

Formula: Ex-showroom price × applicable tax rate % = Road Tax payable.

Example: Car priced at ₹10 lakh with 8% rate → Road tax = ₹80,000.

Always use ex-showroom price, not on-road price, for calculation.

EV Road Tax Exemption in Haryana 2026

Under the Haryana Electric Vehicle Policy 2022 (notified 10 July 2022, valid 5 years till July 2027), EVs registered in Haryana are fully exempt from one-time road tax. This applies to all private EVs — two-wheelers and four-wheelers. Hybrid vehicles do NOT get full exemption; partial concession may apply.

Note: Check current notification on haryanatransport.gov.in before purchase.

Road Tax on Hybrid Cars in Haryana

Hybrid vehicles in Haryana are taxed at a concessional rate compared to petrol/diesel vehicles. Strong hybrids (self-charging, no external plug) typically attract 50% of standard road tax. Plug-in hybrids (PHEVs) may qualify for full EV exemption if registered under the EV policy period. Verify at nearest RTO.

Two-Wheeler Taxation

Two-wheelers in Haryana are taxed at 4% for models under ₹2 lakh and 6% for premium models above ₹2 lakh. Registration fee is ₹300 and HSRP is ₹200. Haryana has a high density of two-wheelers, especially in NCR towns where they serve as convenient commuter vehicles.
